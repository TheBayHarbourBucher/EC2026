ℹ️ About this project

Why student stress matters

Stressed is a Year 11 and 12 Enterprise Computing project exploring student wellbeing through data, interactive tools and education.

The project

What is Stressed?

Stressed is an interactive educational website designed to raise awareness about student stress, burnout and wellbeing within high school environments. The project focuses primarily on students in Years 11 and 12, where academic pressure, assessment tasks, examinations, poor sleep habits and excessive screen time can significantly impact mental health and learning outcomes.

The purpose of the system is to provide students with an engaging platform where they can explore stress-related statistics, reflect on their own lifestyle habits and access practical wellbeing and productivity support resources. Rather than presenting information as large blocks of formal text, the website uses interactive features, visual charts and a stress calculator to make important wellbeing information more accessible and engaging for a student audience.

The problem

Why does this matter?

In modern educational environments many high school students experience high levels of stress and burnout due to academic pressure, assessment tasks, examinations, social pressures, excessive screen time and poor sleep habits. These factors can negatively affect students' mental health, productivity, wellbeing and academic performance.

Students in Years 11 and 12 are especially affected because of increasing workloads and pressure surrounding future education and career pathways. Research consistently shows that many students do not recognise the early signs of stress or understand how unhealthy daily habits can contribute to burnout over time.

Goals

What this website aims to do

The system aims to help students understand the causes and effects of stress while providing practical tools and resources to support healthier study-life balance habits.

1
Raise awareness
Help students understand the scale and impact of stress and burnout in high school environments through real Australian research data.
2
Present real data
Display research on sleep, screen time and academic pressure in an accessible visual format that students can quickly understand and connect to their own lives.
3
Provide an interactive tool
Give students a way to reflect on their own habits and stress levels through the interactive stress calculator quiz with personalised results and tips.
4
Share practical strategies
Provide evidence-based wellbeing and productivity tips covering sleep, study techniques, screen time management and stress management that students can realistically apply.
5
Promote wellbeing awareness
Encourage students to view mental wellbeing as an important part of academic success rather than something separate from their studies.
Audience

Who is this for?

🎓
Year 11 & 12 students
The primary audience. Senior students facing exam pressure, ATAR expectations and workload stress who may benefit most from the data and tools on this site.
📚
University students
Secondary audience. Many of the issues explored — workload, burnout, sleep deprivation and screen time — are equally common in tertiary education environments.
👨‍👩‍👧
Parents & educators
The statistics and wellbeing information on this site may also be useful for parents and teachers who want to better understand and support students experiencing stress.
