Wellbeing & Productivity

Tips that actually work.

Small daily habits can dramatically reduce stress and improve focus. No overhauling your life — just simple, practical changes that compound over time.

😴 Sleep & Recovery
ZZZ
Most important
😴
Sleep is your superpower.

Your brain consolidates memory, regulates mood and repairs itself during sleep. Skipping even one hour affects your concentration, decision-making and emotional resilience the next day.

  • Set a consistent bedtime — even on weekends
  • Aim for 8–10 hours every night — not just on school holidays
  • Keep your room cool, dark and quiet for better sleep quality
  • Avoid caffeine after 2pm — it stays in your system for 6+ hours

No screens before bed
  • Blue light delays melatonin production by up to 90 minutes
  • Put your phone in another room 30–60 min before sleep
  • Try reading or listening to music instead
📚 Study Techniques
25
🍅
Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute blocks then take a 5-minute break. After 4 rounds rest for 20–30 minutes.

  • Prevents mental fatigue and burnout
  • Makes big tasks feel manageable
  • Use a timer app to stay on track
🧠
Active Recall

Test yourself instead of passively re-reading. Retrieval practice is the most effective study method.

  • Use flashcards — Anki is free and excellent
  • Close your notes and write what you remember
  • Teach the concept out loud to yourself
📅
Plan your week

Spend 10 minutes every Sunday mapping tasks to specific days. Removes the stress of not knowing what to do next.

  • Break assignments into daily steps
  • Tackle hardest work when energy is highest
  • Review and adjust the plan each day

  • Use Screen Time (iPhone) or Digital Wellbeing (Android)
  • Cap social media at 1–2 hours per day
  • Turn off non-essential notifications

  • Notice when scrolling makes you feel worse, not better
  • Replace the habit with a 5-minute walk or stretch
  • Unfollow accounts that increase anxiety or comparison
🧘 Stress Management
🏃
Move your body

Exercise is one of the most effective stress reducers. Even a short walk lowers cortisol levels noticeably.

  • Aim for 30–60 min of activity most days
  • A walk between study sessions counts
  • Movement also improves sleep quality
🌬️
Box breathing

A simple technique that calms your nervous system in under 2 minutes. Use it before exams or stressful moments.

  • Inhale for 4 counts
  • Hold for 4 counts
  • Exhale for 4 counts — repeat 4 times
💬
Talk to someone

You don't have to manage stress alone. Talking reduces its emotional weight — even just saying it out loud helps.

  • Friend, parent, teacher or school counsellor
  • Kids Helpline — 1800 55 1800 (free, 24/7)
  • Beyond Blue — 1300 22 4636
