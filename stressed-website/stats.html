📚 Study Stress

Academic pressure peaks in Year 12

Study-related stress climbs sharply as students progress through high school, reaching its highest point in Year 12. This is largely driven by exam pressure, assignment loads and uncertainty about future pathways.

Key finding: Stress levels increase significantly between Year 9 and Year 12, suggesting that pressure accumulates year on year without adequate support structures in place.

😴 Sleep Deprivation

Most students are running on empty

The recommended sleep duration for teenagers is 8–10 hours per night. Yet the average Year 12 student gets significantly less — nearly hours short of what their brain needs to consolidate memory, regulate mood and perform academically.

Key finding: Sleep deprivation compounds stress — students who sleep less than 6 hours are significantly more likely to report feeling burnt out compared to those who sleep 8+ hours.

📱 Screen Time

Students spend more time on screens than sleeping

The average Australian teenager spends hours per day on screens — more time than they spend asleep. Only a fraction of those hours are spent on schoolwork. Social media alone accounts for a significant portion of daily usage, often displacing sleep, exercise and face-to-face social connection.

Key finding: Students who spend more than 5 hours on recreational screens daily are significantly more likely to report poor mental health outcomes compared to those who limit recreational use to under 2 hours.

🔥 Anxiety & Burnout

Burnout is more common than most people realise

Academic burnout is not just tiredness — it is a state of chronic stress that leads to emotional exhaustion, reduced performance and disengagement from school. Many Year 11 and 12 students show key burnout symptoms, yet most do not seek help.

Key finding: Students experiencing burnout are significantly more likely to miss school days and report that their grades have dropped over the past semester.
